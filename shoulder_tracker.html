Daily movement warnings

Tap to acknowledge · these add up even when they feel harmless
😴
Don't sleep on your left side
Hours of shoulder compression overnight undoes recovery. Sleep right side or back. If you wake stiff — this is why.
✓
🎒
No bag on left shoulder
A shoulder bag or strap on the left = sustained isometric load, same pattern as snooker bridging. Switch to right or wear as backpack.
✓
☝️
Overhead left arm — go slow
Reaching above shoulder height with the left arm loads the anterior deltoid. Deliberate slow reach is fine. Quick grab-and-pull is not.
✓
↗️
Avoid reaching across your body
Left hand reaching to the right side (passenger seat, across a table) mirrors the painful diagonal pattern from your resistance test. Use your right hand where possible.
✓
🚪
Prefer right arm for push movements
Pushing doors, trolleys, pushing up off the sofa — all anterior deltoid load. Not dramatic individually, but they add up across a day.
✓
💻
Support left elbow at the desk
Rounded forward posture keeps the anterior deltoid in a shortened position for hours. Rest the left elbow on the desk surface. Shoulder blade squeeze every hour.
✓
⚡
Watch for reactive left arm movements
Quick grab to catch something falling, instinctive reach — unexpected load on a guarded muscle is how strains get aggravated. Build awareness, not paranoia.
✓
Why the rehab exercises work — the deposit mechanism
Offloading: pull-aparts and face pulls train the posterior shoulder to carry stabilisation load — so the strained anterior deltoid gets less daily demand just from existing.
Tissue signal: controlled sub-painful loading tells the body to lay down organised collagen repair. Complete rest produces weaker, disorganised healing.
Inhibition reversal: the nervous system is deliberately limiting how hard the anterior deltoid fires (hence 50% asymmetry). Consistent gentle exercise gradually lifts this protective brake.
