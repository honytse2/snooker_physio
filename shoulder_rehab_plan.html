Phase 1 exercises
1
Band pull-apart
Daily · Phase 1
+
Hold a light band at chest height, arms straight forward. Pull the band apart horizontally until it touches your chest, squeezing your shoulder blades together. Return slowly. Arms stay straight — no elbow bend.
Front view · the work happens in the back of the shoulder, not the front
Common mistake
Arms drifting upward above shoulder height. This loads the front deltoid — exactly what we're avoiding. Keep arms horizontal at chest height the entire rep.
Rear deltoid Mid trapezius Rhomboids Posterior rotator cuff
You should feel

Stop if: burning in front of the left shoulder — band too heavy or arms have drifted up.

Face pull
Daily · Phase 1
+
Anchor a band at face height. Hold both ends with palms facing down. Pull toward your face, flaring elbows out and up. Hands finish beside your ears — not your chin. The end-position external rotation is the key.
Front view · the elbow position is what makes this work
Common mistake
Elbows dropping below shoulder height — this turns the face pull into a row, loads the front deltoid, and skips the rear delt work entirely. Elbows must stay up.
Rear deltoid External rotators Mid + lower trapezius Infraspinatus
You should feel
Back of the shoulders working hard. Slight burn between the shoulder blades. The external rotation at end (hands flaring back beside ears) should add a distinct squeeze.
Stop if: elbows drop or front of shoulder activates — anchor too low or band too heavy.

Diagonal band lift Phase 2 only
When pain-free
+
Step on the band with your right foot. Left hand starts at right hip, palm facing inward. Raise diagonally with arm straight — finishing overhead and slightly to the left, palm rotating to face outward. Lower slowly. This is the D2 flexion pattern.
Front view · diagonal across the midline, palm rotates throughout
Anterior deltoid (left) Serratus anterior Upper trapezius Supraspinatus
You should feel
Dull, controlled fatigue building in the front of the left shoulder as the arm rises. Smooth throughout — no clicking, no sharp catches at any point in the arc.
Do not start until: pull-aparts and face pulls feel easy AND shoulder is pain-free at rest. Sharp pain = return to phase 1.

Push-up modifications

Keep training — change the angle

Push-ups directly load the anterior deltoid. The body angle determines how much.

Side view · same exercise, three angles, very different shoulder loads
Switch to incline push-ups (hands on bench or table)
Wall push-ups if even incline causes pain. Progress slowly: wall → incline → floor over weeks.
Use a wider hand position than normal
Hands at or slightly outside shoulder width. Wider = more chest, less deltoid.
Cut volume to ~50% of normal reps
Left shoulder is working disproportionately hard due to the strength deficit.
Watch for left shoulder rising toward your ear mid-rep
Upper trap compensating. Film yourself side-on or use a mirror.
Avoid diamond / narrow grip push-ups entirely
Hands close = maximum anterior deltoid recruitment. The worst variation right now.

Daily self-checks

  • No anterior deltoid sensation during pull-aparts or face pulls — if you feel the front shoulder, the band is too heavy.
  • Visible muscle size means nothing — the 50% strength asymmetry in the diagonal test is the honest signal.
  • Snooker: avoid long grinding sessions until phase 2. Bridge arm fatigue corrupts your cue feedback without you noticing.
  • If resting pain increases over the week (not just post-exercise soreness), see a physio.
  • Phase 2 gate: pull-aparts and face pulls feel easy AND shoulder pain-free at rest AND during daily movement. All three required.
